\section*{Abstract}\label{abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Abstract}

This note documents a reduced CMS 2012 Open Data search for Higgs boson
decays to tau pairs in the mu tau\_h final state. An optimized
calibrated-score model is trained after multivariate input reweighting
derived before training. On the full data it gives
\texttt{mu\ =\ 38.3802\ +7.0346\ -6.1157} with the upper interval
finite, and an observed 95\% CLs limit
\texttt{mu\ \textless{}\ 50.0000}. This optimized-score result fails the
observed validation gate (\texttt{fail}), so the previous visible-mass
result is retained as the validated baseline: mu = 0.4382 +4.9443
-0.4382; 95\% CLs mu \textless{} 10.7645; median expected 95\% CLs mu
\textless{} 10.7375.

\section*{Change Log}\label{change-log}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Change Log}

Phase 5 v3 updates the final result to the best available trained
classifier after applying data/MC input reweighting before training. It
retains the previous visible-mass result as a frozen baseline for
\texttt{mu} comparison and documents the absence of embedded and EWK Z
reduced samples.

\section{Data And Simulation}\label{data-and-simulation}

The data are Run2012B and Run2012C TauPlusX reduced samples from the
public HiggsTauTauReduced mirror, using the CMS Open Data H to tau tau
luminosity reference of 11.467/fb (CMS Collaboration 2021; CMS Open Data
Analyses 2021). The localized mirror files contain about one tenth of
the event entries listed in the Open Data records; this is treated as
the public reduced processing sample scope, while MC weights continue to
use the official record denominators specified in
\texttt{normalization\_inputs.json}, as required by the sample
provenance.

The available MC set contains ggH and VBF H to tau tau, DYJetsToLL,
TTbar, and W1/W2/W3JetsToLNu. QCD multijet, diboson, single top,
embedded Z to tau tau, W4/inclusive W+jets, associated Higgs, and H to
WW remain unavailable as reduced samples and are not silently
substituted.

\section{Event Selection And
Categories}\label{event-selection-and-categories}

Events are required to pass
\texttt{HLT\_IsoMu17\_eta2p1\_LooseIsoPFTau20}. Muons have pt
\textgreater{} 20 GeV and \textbar eta\textbar{} \textless{} 2.1 with
tight identification and isolation. Hadronic tau candidates have pt
\textgreater{} 20 GeV, \textbar eta\textbar{} \textless{} 2.3,
decay-mode ID, tight isolation ID, and tight anti-muon ID. The signal
region uses opposite-sign mu tau\_h pairs with muon-MET transverse mass
below 40 GeV. The W control region uses high transverse mass, and the
QCD/fake estimate uses same-sign low-mT events.

The fit categories are mutually exclusive VBF, boosted, and zero-jet.
VBF requires at least two clean jets, leading-dijet mass above 300 GeV,
and leading-dijet \textbar Delta eta\textbar{} above 2.5. Boosted events
have at least one clean jet after failing VBF, and zero-jet events have
no clean jets.

\section{QCD And W Control
Corrections}\label{qcd-and-w-control-corrections}

The W+jets normalization is measured in the high-mT control region as
\texttt{0.8528\ ±\ 0.0370}. The 10\% validation value was
\texttt{0.8433\ ±\ 0.0868}. The values agree within the larger 10\%
uncertainty and support the W-control arithmetic.

The VBF category originally had a large MC overprediction. Tightening
the VBF cuts and applying a simple b-tag veto did not solve it, so a
disjoint VBF-like top-btag control region outside the low-mT signal
region was used to calibrate the MC-background rate in the VBF category.
The resulting VBF background scale is \texttt{0.5571\ ±\ 0.0515}; it is
applied only to MC backgrounds in VBF and not to the signal or QCD/fake
template.

The same-sign QCD/fake estimate subtracts non-QCD MC from same-sign
low-mT data and transfers the resulting template to opposite-sign signal
candidates. For the primary calibrated-score model, the OS/SS transfer
factor measured in the lowest score bin is \texttt{0.0204\ ±\ 0.2533}.

Large control-region data/MC differences in several classifier inputs
are handled before training by a multivariate density-ratio reweighting
of background MC. The reweighting classifier is trained only in
non-signal validation/control regions and is then applied to background
MC in the signal-region classifier training and final score templates.
The reduced file set contains no embedded Z to tau tau or EWK Z reduced
sample, so DY/Z is not silently substituted; instead its normalization
is loosened to 30\% in boosted/zero-jet and 50\% in VBF.

\section{Optimized Score Result And
Baseline}\label{optimized-score-result-and-baseline}

The primary statistical model is a binned pyhf profile likelihood in the
calibrated classifier score, with one signal-strength parameter
\texttt{mu}, the W high-mT scale, the same-sign QCD/fake transfer
uncertainty, luminosity, widened DY/Z and tau/open-data normalization
terms, and MC statistical terms (Heinrich et al. 2021; Read 2002; Cowan
et al. 2011).

attempted optimized model for comparison.

\section{Comparison With Published
Results}\label{comparison-with-published-results}

CMS Run 1 reported evidence for H to tau tau using 7 and 8 TeV data with
multiple final states, embedded backgrounds, and a full calibration
program; the quoted best-fit signal strength was 0.78 ± 0.27 with
observed and expected significances of 3.2 and 3.7 standard deviations
(CMS Collaboration 2014). CMS 2018 reported observed and expected
significances of 4.9 and 4.7 in the 2016 data, and 5.9 when combined
with earlier CMS data, with a signal strength near 1.09 (CMS
Collaboration 2018). The ATLAS+CMS Run 1 combination gives broader
global Higgs-rate context, while the PDG/Higgs-summary SM H to tau tau
branching fraction provides the denominator convention for
\texttt{mu\ =\ 1} rather than a single-channel open-data comparison
target (ATLAS Collaboration and CMS Collaboration 2016; {Navas et al.}
2024; Particle Data Group 2024; LHC Higgs Cross Section Working Group
2017).

\section{Conclusion}\label{conclusion}

The optimized calibrated-score fit was attempted after pre-training
multivariate input reweighting, but it fails the observed validation
gate. It is therefore compared against, not substituted for, the frozen
visible-mass baseline. The baseline gives mu = 0.4382 +4.9443 -0.4382;
95\% CLs mu \textless{} 10.7645; median expected 95\% CLs mu \textless{}
10.7375. This remains a reduced Open Data diagnostic, not CMS-quality
evidence for H to tau tau.
