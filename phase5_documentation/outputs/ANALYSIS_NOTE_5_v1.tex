\section*{Abstract}\label{abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Abstract}

This note documents a reduced CMS 2012 Open Data search for Higgs boson
decays to tau pairs in the mu tau\_h final state. A Phase 5 audit found
that the original observed BDT-score fit was not a valid evidence
result: it omitted a reducible QCD/fake-tau background estimate and
failed high-score shape validation. The final conservative result
therefore uses visible mass in VBF, boosted, and zero-jet categories
with a same-sign data-driven QCD/fake template. It gives
\texttt{mu\_hat\ =\ 0.4382}, an observed 95\% CLs limit
\texttt{mu\ \textless{}\ 10.7645}, and \texttt{Z\ =\ 0.0949}. The
categorized BDT-score model is retained as a flagged diagnostic with
\texttt{mu\_hat\ =\ 9.3518} and \texttt{Z\ =\ 3.9416}.

\needspace{4\baselineskip}
\section*{Change Log}\label{change-log}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Change Log}

Phase 5 v2 responds to the observed-versus-expected audit. It adds a
same-sign QCD/fake estimate, changes the primary full-data result to the
visible-mass fallback, keeps the BDT score as a diagnostic, adds NN/MVA

\section{Data And Simulation}\label{data-and-simulation}

The data are Run2012B and Run2012C TauPlusX reduced samples from the
public HiggsTauTauReduced mirror, using the CMS Open Data H to tau tau
luminosity reference of 11.467/fb (CMS Collaboration 2021; CMS Open Data
Analyses 2021). The localized mirror files contain about one tenth of
the event entries listed in the Open Data records; this is treated as
the public reduced processing sample scope, while MC weights continue to
use the official record denominators specified in
\texttt{normalization\_inputs.json}, as required by the sample
provenance.

The available MC set contains ggH and VBF H to tau tau, DYJetsToLL,
TTbar, and W1/W2/W3JetsToLNu. QCD multijet, diboson, single top,
embedded Z to tau tau, W4/inclusive W+jets, associated Higgs, and H to
WW remain unavailable as reduced samples and are not silently
substituted.

\section{Event Selection And
Categories}\label{event-selection-and-categories}

Events are required to pass
\texttt{HLT\_IsoMu17\_eta2p1\_LooseIsoPFTau20}. Muons have pt
\textgreater{} 20 GeV and \textbar eta\textbar{} \textless{} 2.1 with
tight identification and isolation. Hadronic tau candidates have pt
\textgreater{} 20 GeV, \textbar eta\textbar{} \textless{} 2.3,
decay-mode ID, tight isolation ID, and tight anti-muon ID. The signal
region uses opposite-sign mu tau\_h pairs with muon-MET transverse mass
below 40 GeV. The W control region uses high transverse mass, and the
QCD/fake estimate uses same-sign low-mT events.

The fit categories are mutually exclusive VBF, boosted, and zero-jet.
VBF requires at least two clean jets, leading-dijet mass above 300 GeV,
and leading-dijet \textbar Delta eta\textbar{} above 2.5. Boosted events
have at least one clean jet after failing VBF, and zero-jet events have
no clean jets.

\section{QCD And W Control
Corrections}\label{qcd-and-w-control-corrections}

The W+jets normalization is measured in the high-mT control region as
\texttt{0.8528\ ±\ 0.0370}. The 10\% validation value was
\texttt{0.8433\ ±\ 0.0868}. The values agree within the larger 10\%
uncertainty and support the W-control arithmetic.

The VBF category originally had a large MC overprediction. Tightening
the VBF cuts and applying a simple b-tag veto did not solve it, so a
disjoint VBF-like top-btag control region outside the low-mT signal
region was used to calibrate the MC-background rate in the VBF category.
The resulting VBF background scale is \texttt{0.5571\ ±\ 0.0515}; it is
applied only to MC backgrounds in VBF and not to the signal or QCD/fake
template.

The same-sign QCD/fake estimate subtracts non-QCD MC from same-sign
low-mT data and transfers the resulting template to opposite-sign signal
candidates. For the primary visible-mass model, the OS/SS transfer
factor measured in the lowest visible-mass bin is
\texttt{0.9958\ ±\ 0.1201}. For the BDT score diagnostic, the
corresponding low-score factor is \texttt{0.8173\ ±\ 0.0853}.

\section{MVA And Transformer
Diagnostics}\label{mva-and-transformer-diagnostics}

The requested NN/MVA program was evaluated using the available reduced
event features. Histogram gradient boosting, an MLP neural network, and
XGBoost were trained on selected MC only. A transformer was not trained
because the current pixi environment lacks a fast attention stack and
the reduced files do not contain the GenMET target needed for the
requested missing-momentum regression.

\section{Primary Visible-Mass Result}\label{primary-visible-mass-result}

high-mT scale, the same-sign QCD/fake transfer uncertainty, luminosity,
DY and tau/open-data normalization terms, and MC statistical terms
(Heinrich et al. 2021; Read 2002; Cowan et al. 2011).

\section{Comparison With Published
Results}\label{comparison-with-published-results}

CMS Run 1 reported evidence for H to tau tau using 7 and 8 TeV data with
multiple final states, embedded backgrounds, and a full calibration
program; the quoted best-fit signal strength was 0.78 ± 0.27 (CMS
Collaboration 2014). CMS 2018 reported observed and expected
significances of 4.9 and 4.7 and a signal strength near 1.09 (CMS
Collaboration 2018). These are not direct pass/fail targets for this
reduced open-data workflow.

\section{Conclusion}\label{conclusion}

The final audit-corrected result is a conservative visible-mass plus
QCD/fake template fit. It gives \texttt{mu\_hat\ =\ 0.4382}, an observed
95\% CLs limit \texttt{mu\ \textless{}\ 10.7645}, and
\texttt{Z\ =\ 0.0949}. The originally surprising BDT-score observed
result is retained only as a flagged diagnostic because the score shape
is not validated in data. This is the correct interpretation of the
reduced CMS Open Data workflow: reproducible and useful for methodology,
but not CMS-quality evidence for H to tau tau.
