\section*{Abstract}\label{abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Abstract}

A diagnostic search for H to tau tau is performed with CMS 2012 Open
Data in the mu tau\_h final state using reduced Run2012B/C TauPlusX
samples. A binned profile-likelihood fit to classifier-score templates
in VBF, boosted, and zero-jet categories gives
\texttt{mu\_hat\ =\ 7.664}, an observed 95\% CLs upper limit
\texttt{mu\ \textless{}\ 13.323}, and an observed q0 diagnostic
\texttt{Z\ =\ 2.978}. The score-template validation is flagged in both
the 10\% validation sample and the full data, so these values are
simplified open-data diagnostics and are not CMS-quality evidence for H
to tau tau.

\needspace{4\baselineskip}
\FloatBarrier
\section{Introduction}\label{introduction}

The H to tau tau decay probes the Higgs coupling to charged leptons. CMS
reported Run 1 evidence for this decay and later observed it with 13 TeV
data (CMS Collaboration 2014, 2018). This paper draft presents a compact
open-data diagnostic using the public reduced CMS 2012 H to tau tau
samples (CMS Collaboration 2021a). The analysis is limited to the mu
tau\_h final state and is intended as a reproducible public-data
workflow, not a replacement for the full CMS publications.

\needspace{4\baselineskip}
\FloatBarrier
\section{Data And Simulation}\label{data-and-simulation}

The data are Run2012B and Run2012C TauPlusX reduced NanoAOD-like samples
from the CERN Open Data Portal (CMS Collaboration 2021d, 2021e). The
integrated luminosity is 11.467/fb, following the CMS Open Data H to tau
tau tutorial and 2012 luminosity provenance (CMS Open Data Analyses
2021; CMS Collaboration 2017). The luminosity uncertainty is 2.6\% (CMS
Collaboration 2013).

\FloatBarrier
\section{Event Selection And
Categories}\label{event-selection-and-categories}

Events are required to pass
\texttt{HLT\_IsoMu17\_eta2p1\_LooseIsoPFTau20}. Muons have pt
\textgreater{} 20 GeV and \textbar eta\textbar{} \textless{} 2.1 with
tight identification and isolation. Hadronic-tau candidates have pt
\textgreater{} 20 GeV, \textbar eta\textbar{} \textless{} 2.3,
decay-mode ID, tight isolation ID, and tight anti-muon ID. The
signal-region selection requires an opposite-sign mu tau\_h pair and
muon-MET transverse mass below 40 GeV.

The fit uses three mutually exclusive categories: VBF, boosted, and
zero-jet. VBF events have at least two clean jets with leading-dijet
mass above 300 GeV and leading-dijet \textbar Delta eta\textbar{} above
2.5. Boosted events have at least one clean jet after failing the VBF
selection, and zero-jet events have no clean jets.

\section{Statistical Analysis}\label{statistical-analysis}

The fitted observable is a histogram-gradient-boosting classifier score
in the three event categories. The likelihood is a binned profile
likelihood implemented with pyhf (Heinrich et al. 2021),

\begin{equation}\protect\phantomsection\label{eq:paper-likelihood}{
L(\mu,\theta)=\prod_{c,b} \mathrm{Pois}\left(n_{cb}\mid
\mu s_{cb}(\theta)+b_{cb}(\theta)\right)
\prod_k \pi_k(\theta_k).
}\end{equation}

Upper limits use the CLs construction (Read 2002), and the discovery
diagnostic uses the one-sided q0 asymptotic approximation (Cowan et al.
2011). The W+jets rate is constrained by a high-mT control-region scale
\texttt{0.8528\ ±\ 0.0370}. The retained nuisances include luminosity,
DY normalization, tau open-data acceptance, W high-mT normalization, and
MC statistical uncertainties.

\FloatBarrier
\section{Validation Caveat}\label{validation-caveat}

The score-template model is not validated at CMS-publication quality. In
the 10\% validation sample, the combined data/background ratio is
\texttt{1.232} with chi2/ndf \texttt{1.732} and status \texttt{flagged}.
In the full data, the combined data/background ratio is \texttt{1.182}
with chi2/ndf \texttt{3.218} and status \texttt{flagged}. Therefore the
observed \texttt{Z\ =\ 2.978} and \texttt{mu\_hat\ =\ 7.664} are
simplified open-data diagnostics, not evidence comparable to CMS
publications.

\section{Comparison With Published
Results}\label{comparison-with-published-results}

The open-data result is compared with CMS H to tau tau publications only
as context. CMS Run 1 used 7 and 8 TeV data with multiple channels and
reported mu = 0.78 ± 0.27 with local significance above three standard
deviations (CMS Collaboration 2014). CMS 2018 used 13 TeV data and
reported observed and expected significances of 4.9 and 4.7 with mu =
1.09 and roughly 0.27 uncertainty (CMS Collaboration 2018). These
publication results are not direct pass/fail targets for the reduced
single-channel open-data model.

\FloatBarrier
\section{Conclusion}\label{conclusion}

A reproducible reduced CMS 2012 Open Data H to tau tau diagnostic search
has been documented in the mu tau\_h final state. The full-data fit
gives \texttt{mu\_hat\ =\ 7.664}, \texttt{mu\ \textless{}\ 13.323} at
95\% CLs, and \texttt{Z\ =\ 2.978}. Because the 10\% and full-data
score-template validations are flagged, these are simplified open-data
diagnostics and not CMS-quality evidence. The analysis is useful as a
public workflow and as a scoped comparison to CMS H to tau tau
publications, not as an independent publication measurement.
